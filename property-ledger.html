It's close, and the two properties win on different things rather than one being clearly better. A's edge is price positioning (bidding at last year's comp price in a still-appreciating area) and a stronger growth story, since Donabate's DART upgrade is still ahead of it — more upside, but further out. B's edge is a meaningfully better day-to-day commute (working DART today vs. a future one) and a more practical layout with the second bathroom. Investment/Growth is the one criterion where "further out" (A) is scored above "already realised" (B) — if you'd rather weight proven infrastructure over future potential, that gap could easily flip. This is genuinely close enough that your own weightings on location vs. growth will likely decide it more than my scores do.
